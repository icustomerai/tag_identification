About BMI

Building Media Inc. is a developer of innovative online learning management programs and e-Learning courseware built exclusively for the construction community. Since 2000, BMI has provided distance education to the entire construction community including: architects, engineers, contractors, remodelers, raters, code officials, product manufacturers and state and federal groups. Today, BMI builds cloud-based, SaaS learning communities and blended courseware designed to engage the learner and track performance, while simultaneously reducing associated educational costs and resource demand.

Our Projects

BMI is a pioneer in distance training, with nearly 15 years of experience in what started out as “computerized learning.” With that foundation, BMI’s programs today are both well-known and highly regarded in the eLearning sphere. Regardless of changing platforms and evolving technology, BMI strives to embrace the best of contemporary platforms, networks, and user devices while also seeking first adapter status for the “next big thing.” As a company exclusively focused on delivering education and training over the internet, we’ve witnessed both the cost and availability of bandwidth go from a scarcity to a commodity, and with that, the delivery of content go from basic text and choppy videos to richly delivered audio visual content and even live, interactive presentations with synchronous blends, real-time polling, and testing and performance based outcomes. Video Training Examples

This hybrid video/photo lesson, hosted by energy expert Eric Makula, was produced for SECO and covers Compliance with the Commercial 2009 International Energy Conservation Code (IECC) The program provides training on how to conduct plan reviews and field inspections to determine compliance with the commercial

requirements of the 2009 IECC. It demonstrates the use of compliance inspection checklists developed by PNNL to assist states in determining if they meet 90% compliance with the code requirements. The course also describes how to conduct a plan review, how to assess compliance with specific code provisions in the field, how to determine overall compliance rates, and how to gather and report data.
