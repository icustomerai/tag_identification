Colgin Cellars

An Uncommon Journey Since 1992

For over three decades, Colgin Cellars has embraced the ideal of crafting wines of harmony, grace, and precision from exceptional hillside vineyards in the heart of the Napa Valley.

Driven by a love for the land and a reverence for the artistic beauty of fine wine, Colgin Cellars is synonymous with elegance, power, and longevity. We have a shared vision of creating lasting memories by capturing a singular expression of time and place.

The Three Pillars of Colgin

The ethos of Colgin Cellars is to create wines of perfume, freshness, and minerality while embracing the diversity of hillside sites in the Napa Valley.

Perfume

Great wines beguile with aromatics that captivate and transport you to the place of their origin.

Freshness

A hallmark of fine wine is the mouth-watering impression that beckons you to experience the next taste.

Minerality

The distinctive geological features in our rocky, hillside vineyards define the soul of the wines. Minerality is the subtle nuance that takes you to the place where vines are grown–a sensation one feels rather than smells or tastes.

A Sense of Place

The Napa region is one of the most diverse places for growing grapes on Earth. In an area that is home to over half of the world’s soil types, there are specific sites that stand out as being world-class in quality and distinct in their nature. The combination of unique geology coupled with mid-slope elevations, and cooling airflow in concert with precision farming, establishes certain sites as the pinnacle of quality for the Napa Valley.

It is our privilege to produce profound wines that magnify the inherent beauty and complexity found in these distinctive hillside vineyards.

Explore Our Vineyards

Tychson Hill

Jewel box vineyard originally planted in the 19th Century

Site: North of the town of St. Helena

Elevation: 300-422 feet (91-129 meters)

Soil: Weathered volcanic rocks with reddish brown stones and obsidian

Cariad

Rocky hillsides overlooking the heart of Napa Valley

Site: Western hills of St. Helena

Elevation: 400-500 feet (122-152 meters)

Soil: Volcanic stony soils with gravelly alluvium

IX Estate

Carved into a steep east facing slope

Site: Pritchard Hill area overlooking Lake Hennessey

Elevation: 1100-1400 feet (335-427 meters)

Soil: Well-drained reddish clays with weathered igneous rocks
