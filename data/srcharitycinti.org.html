Seton Garden Dedicated

Sisters, Associates, employees and friends of the Community attended the blessing of the new Seton Garden on Thursday, June 8, 2023. The short prayer service included the dedication of memorial trees for Jim Franz (former director of Plant Operations) and Associate Rita Wesseling. The Seton Garden is located in the Seton Hall footprint, and is a native pollinator garden that provides a habitat for native and migrating birds, pollinators and other insects. Walking paths and seating throughout invite visitors to enjoy this serene space.

Sisters of Charity Celebrate World Environment

The Sisters of Charity invited Sisters, Associates and friends of the Community to celebrate World Environment Day on Monday, June 5, 2023 at La Casa del Sol Ministry Center. The evening included the opportunity to see the Community’s newest sustainable home constructed with a number of state-of-the-art energy conserving technologies. Sisters and Associates presented on a variety of Earth-related topics, reflecting on how we can live more responsibly in our daily lives. Participant conversation and door prizes were incorporated into the programming, as well. The event was sponsored by the Earth Justice Circle, a ministry project of the Sisters of Charity of Cincinnati.

Congratulations

Join us in congratulating Associate Patsy Schwaiger upon receiving the St. Elizabeth Ann Seton Mission Award from Mount St. Joseph University. According to the Mount, “Patsy has given generously of her time and talent to share the story of Mother Margaret George, one of the founding members of the Cincinnati community, during every new student orientation. Patsy has been an active participant in the Charity Learning Community since its inception. She has a deep devotion to St. Elizabeth Ann Seton, founder of the Sisters of Charity, and the heritage and legacy of the community. She is an active Associate member of the Sisters of Charity.”

The St. Elizabeth Ann Seton Mission Award was established in 2001 and is the highest mission honor bestowed by the university; the award recognizes those who live out the mission day after day in an exemplary manner. Individuals honored distinguish themselves by “daring to risk a caring response” in their service to the Mount community and beyond.

S. Anthony O’Connell Recognized

The Clough Valley Chapter of the Daughters of the American Revolution, on the eastside of Cincinnati, honored S. Anthony O’Connell with the National Society Daughters of the American Revolution (NSDAR) award as a “Woman in American History.” Representatives from the Chapter recognized S. Anthony on Thursday, June 1, 2023, with a certificate and a Woman of American History Medal. A small group of Sisters and Associates were present for the presentation in the Seton Garden.
