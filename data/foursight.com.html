Big Bend National Park

Big Bend National Park is a United States National Park located in West Texas, bordering Mexico. It has national significance as the largest protected area of Chihuahuan Desert topography and ecology in the United States. It contains more than 1,200 species of plants, more than 450 species of birds, 56 species of reptiles, and 75 species of mammals.[3]

Monument Valley

The park, frequently a filming location for Western movies, is accessed by the looping, 17-mile Valley Drive. Q. How do I make payments on my loan?
Automatic (Reoccurring) Payment: Sign into your account online. Go to the Autopay Setup tab. Check the Enabled button to enable the draft authorization. Input payment information requested and indicate if funds are to draft from checking or savings. Click Submit. Review Autopay terms for accuracy. This will setup payments to be automatically debited from your checking or savings account on a monthly, reoccurring, basis. There is no charge for this service. One-time direct debit payment: You may make a payment online by requesting a one-time debit from your bank account, for a nominal fee. To use this service sign into your account online. Click on the Make A Payment tab. Select payment method and click Next. Input payment information requested and indicate if funds are to draft from checking or savings. Indicate that the terms have been read and accepted and click the Submit button. By phone: Call us at 1-888-960-8063 to make a payment by phone. Phone payments may be made by check or debit card for a nominal fee.
Q. How do I get a current payoff amount for my loan?
Your payoff balance can be viewed online by logging into your account. The balance quoted is good for ten days to allow time for the payoff to be received and posted to your account.
