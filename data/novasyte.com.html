Contract CNE Teams

A $900 million global leader in the wound care market had the opportunity to compete and win a dual-source contract (valued at $19 million annually) with a large IDN health system. Our customer was in need of a team of Clinical Nurse Educators (CNE) to assist with the end user clinical demands.

Customer Situation

Our client, a $900 million global leader in the wound care market, had the opportunity to compete and win a dual-source contract (valued at $19 million annually) with a large IDN health system. In order to effectively manage the trial, our customer was in need of a team of Clinical Nurse Educators (CNE) to assist with the end user clinical demands. The team would be led by the sales force and interact with both the trial sites, the clinicians utilizing the products and the patients who were being treated. The trial would last over 6 months and would span from Hawaii to Atlanta.

Novasyte Analysis

Before recommending a turnkey solution, Novasyte worked with senior leadership to understand the customers’ (IDN’s) expectations, the geographic regions of interest, the ideal background of candidates and milestones set that would achieve success. This process included interviews with the regional managers involved in the trial, an analysis of the current sales process, mapping the flow of the product from prescription to implementation and a detailed discussion of key lessons learned from previous successes and failures.

Novasyte Recommendation

In order to provide coverage for all locations participating in the trial and to keep the sales force to stay in the field selling, Novasyte recruited a team of six wound care specialists strategically located in “hotbed” areas with a dedicated weekly commitment of 20 hours per CNE. Novasyte utilized its proprietary Virtual Interview Software (VIS) to initially screen all candidates and then shortlisted that group for face-to-face meetings with local managers. Once the Novasyte team of CNEs had been identified, Novasyte implemented its “Boot Camp Training Platform”:

1) Novasyte Learning Management System (LMS) - eLearning modules provide baseline training on the industry, completion and products
2) 1 day in-house customer sponsored training event at Novasyte’s Southern California office
3) 1 week in field training with local rep and Clinical Resource Specialist


In addition to the CNE team, Novasyte provided a dedicated Project Manager and Administrator. This team managed all scheduling, weekly contact reports, monthly reviews, back office solutions, payroll, tax filing, expense management and team communication.

Business Impact

Novasyte’s contract CNE team provided our client with the resources needed to conduct a flawless trial. Because this was a trial and not a conversion, our customer was very cognizant of managing both the customers’ needs and the internal costs. In addition, Novasyte’s program allowed the sales force to stay in the field selling while the trial took place. Our client has been so happy with the results of this particular trial that they have asked Novasyte to propose a national rollout of our Contract CNE Team solution.

Novasyte has transformed the way we do business today.
President, Global wound care company

Just-In-Time CNE Teams

A Fortune 500 leader in the medical device space was dissatisfied with the quality of their current “generalist” clinical staffing firm. Novasyte created a tailor-made solution to assist in all clinical education needs.

Customer Situation

Our client, a $10 billion global leader in the medical device and supply space, had collaborated with a large publicly traded staffing firm to provide all of their staffing needs (forklift operators, accountants, administrators, etc.). In addition to these areas, the staffing organization attempted to provide Clinical Nurse Educators (CNEs) as a resource to assist in the conversion of new products. Our client experienced mixed results in regards to quality of candidates, preparedness and viability as “customer facing” brand representatives. In order to meet the ever-increasing clinical demands of their product portfolio, our client asked Novasyte to analyze the current CNE needs, build a national CNE team, and manage deployment on a conversion-by-conversion basis.

Novasyte Analysis

Novasyte began its initial exploration by interviewing the individual division managers, area managers, and regional managers to understand the clinical needs of each product being implemented. In addition, Novasyte examined the current methods and where they were breaking down. From these discussions, Novasyte focused on developing and deploying a strategic plan that focused on the needs of the product and the geography.

Novasyte Recommendation

With a focus on key geographic hotspots, specialized CNE backgrounds, and concise onboarding, Novasyte recommended a “blended” clinical solution. This “blended” solution consisted of both “Just-in-Time” local CNEs used in conjunction with a “dedicated” team of CNEs who worked a minimum of 20 hours a week. The strategic delivery was inclusive of:

1) Improve Process Ordering
2) Improve Candidates Screening (”Brand Representative Model”)
3) Detailed Onboarding Process
4) Improve Training Initiatives
5) Manage Deployment/Scheduling Service

Business Impact

Today, Novasyte provides a transparent CNE team that is designed with both the customer and the sales force in mind. Novasyte has hit key milestones over the 1.5 years we have partnered with this client, including fill ratio, time to fill and “brand representative” identification. With a finely tuned CNE profile, a custom designed Learning Management System (LMS), and a proprietary scheduling tool, Novasyte has provided over 150 hospital conversions, converting over $15 million in new business. In addition to new business converted, Novasyte’s CNE team has defended over $3 million in existing business and provided countless “refresher” courses for end users all while minimizing the FTE cost center.

“Novasyte’s custom clinical support solution has allowed us to hit our goals year over year by freeing up the sales force.”
Vice President of Sales, Global medical device company

Full Time CNE Teams

A global leader in the ostomy, wound care and incontinence industry partnered with Novasyte to assist in launching a phase II product extension that focused exclusively on the pediatric market.

Customer Situation

Our client, a global leader in the ostomy, wound care and incontinence industry was in the process of launching a phase II product extension that focused exclusively on the pediatric market. Developed in-house and armed with a solid marketing plan, our client was wary of adding FTEs at the CNE level in order to drive adoption. Wanting to give the product launch every chance of success, our client was in need of a “dedicated” clinical solution to assist the sales force (in specific areas) in educating end users during the sales process and post-acquisition on the value of this niche product.

Novasyte Analysis

Novasyte was brought into our client’s pre-launch discussions with an emphasis on understanding the clinical complications and hurdles experienced when converting a new customer. Surveying the regional management team and sales force, Novasyte understood that this particular product required many dedicated hours to assist the end users in full adoption and usage. In addition, it was evident that the CNE team would need to work in lockstep with the sales force in every step of the process (initial introduction, trial, post-trial results delivery and conversion).

Novasyte Recommendation

Knowing that our customer wanted to launch this product in one particular market (Northeast) and control the launch closely, Novasyte proposed a “Full-Time” CNE solution that would provide all the benefits of an FTE resource without the necessary budget approvals, liabilities, fixed costs, and long-term commitments. Novasyte recruited, screened and deployed one CNE in the Northeast on a six-month Novasyte contract, working directly for our customer. The option of expanding the program nationally based on the success of the product launch has been established as Phase II.

1) Candidate Recruitment/Identification
2) Detailed Screening (Virtual Interview Software (VIS))
3) Employment Verification/Background Check
4) Weekly Call Reporting/Management
5) All Necessary Employment Armin. (Insurances/Credentialing/Payroll/Tax Filing/Expense Management)

Business Impact

When the initial product was ready to be piloted, our customer was concerned with adding fixed costs to the launch without truly understanding the market potential. In order to mitigate risk, our client was able to launch their niche product in a pilot market with a “dedicated” CNE resource to support and manage the rollout. Since going live, our CNE has been present on all pre, mid, and post-trial meetings and has participated in driving adoption in the Northeast. Today, our client has leveraged Novasyte across the country to support multiple product launches with a flexible clinical support team that scales up and down as the product is adopted.

By utilizing Novasyte, we have been able to scale our product launches with minimal effort.

Large Scale Product Conversion

A Fortune 500 medical device client was tasked with implementing a 42 hospital IDN system away from a heavily entrenched competitor. The IDN placed significant time constraints around the conversion time and mandated 24/7 support.

Customer Situation

Our client, a Fortune 500 medical device organization was awarded a $7 million new product conversion. The incumbent product had been in the system for over 15 years and had a tremendous amount of legacy support. As a condition of the agreement, our client had to commit to converting all 42 hospitals in less than 60 days. Not wanting to pull the entire sales force into the conversion process for 60 days, Novasyte was chosen to provide a flexible clinical support solution made up of 35 Novasyte consultants that would work in conjunction with the full-time field-based team.

Novasyte Analysis

Novasyte collaborated closely with the senior leadership on understanding the IDN’s expectations, the geographic areas needed to be supported, the timelines for going live in each account, and the schedules necessary to accomplish the allocated budget. Working with a cross functional team on a bi-weekly basis, Novasyte and the client built the plan.

Novasyte Recommendation

The key to executing the plan inside of the 60-day limit was to have multiple conversions take place at the same time. With an orchestrated approach, Novasyte’s team had to work in conjunction with the customer’s sales force to effectively build a proposal for each of the 42 hospitals, articulate and gain acceptance on the plan from the hospital, and then execute against what was agreed upon. All of this was accomplished with multiple facilities coordinated at one time.

Business Impact

Coming in under budget and on time, Novasyte supported our client with a flexible, scalable team of 35 clinical consultants. The IDN considered this particular conversion to be one of the “smoothest” in their history. Partnering with Novasyte allowed the sales force to stay in the field selling while the conversions took place. In addition to increasing sales force selling time, Novasyte was able to decrease the time to revenue recognition and decrease the cost of conversion.

National Field Corrective Action

Customer Situation

Our client, a Fortune 500 medical device manufacturer needed additional resources for a large voluntary field corrective action. They were tasked with a necessary software revision of approximately 17,000 ventilators. With a timeline of nine months, our client needed to execute the FCA quickly and efficiently with all their customers across the U.S.

Novasyte Analysis

Novasyte strategized with leadership to determine the key influencing factors of the program, including available resources, timelines, budget, expense management, asset tracking and data management. Novasyte concluded that reaching six to eight units per day would achieve the targeted cost per unit of $150. A budget of $1.5M was established to meet this goal.

Novasyte Recommendation

After calculating the estimated cost and the requirements to complete the upgrade, Novasyte deployed 15 biomedical technicians in multiple hospitals and IDNs across the US. The team were scheduled, deployed and managed by Novasyte throughout the project. Novasyte reported their progress to the client daily on upgrades completed and data entry for the FDA.

Business Impact

Novasyte was able to successfully build a team that would complete the ventilator upgrade under budget and in seven months versus the original nine-month estimate. With our account management teams, travel program, and logistics management, Novasyte was able to manage client expenses within budgetary limits.

After-Hours Phone Support for New Product Launch

Customer Situation

Our Client, a global leader in the medical device industry, targets sleep-disordered breathing, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease, and other chronic diseases. They have been pioneering new and innovative devices and treatments for more than 25 years. This client has recently launched a new ventilator that provides invasive and non-invasive ventilation for patients weighing more than 11 lbs. This new product is primarily utilized in the extended care market. Novasyte has partnered with them to provide additional patient and caregiver support for this product launch.

Novasyte Analysis & Recommendation

As our client commercialized this ventilator, Novasyte identified a gap in their support coverage. They were equipped internally to handle incoming technical and clinical calls during daytime hours with their current staff. However, due to the ventilators’ nature as a life-support device, our client was challenged with having the necessary bandwidth with this same team to cover support calls after-hours and on weekends. Novasyte understands the importance of collecting data around call times, call volume, and most importantly successful outcomes, so a plan was created to support this initiative.

Novasyte built a team of eight Certified Respiratory Therapists (CRT) that were cross-trained on both the technical and clinical aspects of the ventilator. Novasyte was responsible for all recruitment, deployment, CRM, phone systems, analytics, and expense management. The Novasyte call center VoIP solution delivered reliable mobile, flexible, affordable and cloud-based communications. The CRM solution captured the data from the CRTs around each patient/care-giver interaction.

Business Impact

Novasyte provided greater bandwidth capability for our client’s internal team for this life-support ventilator. The overall business impact was as follows:

> 7,000 hours of annual clinical phone support
> 60% less time burden on client resources
> 24/7 clinical hotline provides a value-added selling feature to potential customers
> Additional flexible field resources available

Increased Selling Time

Customer Situation

Our client, a Fortune 500 medical device manufacturer, needed to downsize 28% of their sales force. They were tasked with continuing to defend their market share with the reduced SG&A budget. This downsizing left them with gaps in certain territories with a need for both sales representation, as well as a clinical presence, to provide support to their customers that would drive product utilization and pull-through.

Novasyte Analysis & Recommendation

Novasyte consulted with client leadership and examined territory alignment of the restructured sales force. From this analysis, there were certain geographies identified that warranted a sales and/or clinical presence. Novasyte identified the top five territories across the U.S. that lacked the revenue to support a full-line sales representative. Additionally, four other territories needed part-time clinical support.

Novasyte deployed five Sales Consultants in the identified territories working under a full-time model. Four Clinical Consultants were deployed on a flexible basis to support the remaining territories. Our client was responsible for daily activity field task management of the consultants. Novasyte was responsible for scheduling, deployment, human resources, payroll functions, and providing detailed analytics reporting on activity and production.

Business Impact

> 28% reduction in SG&A cost for client
> 106.1% sales quota attainment by Novasyte Sales Consultant team
> Client maintained recurring baseline business
> Client kept competitive threats from gaining market share
