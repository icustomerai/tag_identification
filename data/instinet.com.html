Agents
of change.

When Instinet gave birth to electronic trading in 1969, we were a small tech-focused upstart with a simple (but then, radical) point of view: that technology can bring great efficiency to the financial markets. No one had coined the term “fintech” yet, but that’s what we were. We’ve made it our mission to remain at the forefront of advancements that shape the financial markets ever since.

Today, as part of Nomura Group, Instinet’s global agency model* provides services that span the entire investment life cycle. Our goal is to help our institutional clients create, preserve and capture alpha in a changing world.

Our Approach

The vision to lead. The capabilities to perform.

We operate with a “client-first” approach. It’s built on robust and adaptive technology and decades of experience fine-tuning our capabilities to a constantly-shifting marketplace and for the evolving objectives of our clients.

Instinet offers the institutional investment community a suite of tailorable solutions, including market insights and commentary, full service trading desks, advanced algorithmic trading strategies, access to unique and specialized liquidity via our array of crossing pools, and a set of award-winning workflow solutions engineered to help our clients efficiently manage their investment process, their way.

Celebrating the first 50 years.
Imagining the next 50.

In 1969, we were upstarts with a radical idea. That idea was an electronic network for trading stocks.

This concept seems like a given now, but fifty years ago it was the beginning of a revolution—a fundamental shift that redefined how the markets work and how investors interact with each other.

The term hadn’t been coined yet, but Instinet was the original “fintech”.

Over the last five decades, we have often had the opportunity to be a positive transformative force in the markets. The innovations we've developed on behalf of our clients have played an integral role in many of the advancements that have shaped how the investment life cycle works today.

That pioneering spirit still drives us, and inspires innovation that improves our clients’ performance, creates more efficient and accessible markets, and effectively supports the investments that benefit people and enterprises all over the world.

What is Fintech? Where did it begin?

The Seventies

1969

Electronic trading is born.
The Instinet Communication System, the market’s first ECN, goes live.

1971

Exchange evolution.
NASDAQ launches the world’s first fully electronic stock market.

1977

Seeing green.
Instinet introduces the first US stock quote montage, the Green Screen.

The Eighties

1984

Electrifying specialists.
NYSE SuperDOT system allows orders to be routed electronically directly to specialists.

1986

First crossing.
Instinet launches the first point-in-time cross in the US.

1987

Hold the phone.
Automated NYSE Small Order Execution System (SOES) is introduced after dealers refused to answer the phone during the 1987 crash.

The Nineties

1992

FIXing everything.
The Financial Information eXchange (FIX) real-time electronic communications protocol is introduced.

1993

Meet the modern OMS.
Instinet OMS is among the earliest order management systems for equities.

1997

Let the revolution begin.
SEC Order Handling Rules remake the competitive structure of the US equity markets, boosting electronic trading and beginning the market’s tech revolution.

1999

Get smart.
As new trading venues fragment the US equity market, Instinet launches Helix, a pioneering smart-routing technology.

The Two Thousands

2001

Technology versus humans.
IBM researchers publish a paper that shows algorithmic strategies consistently outperforming human traders. Meanwhile, the US dot-com bubble is bursting.

2005

Going dark.
SEC National Market Structure (NMS) regulation is implemented, driving more volume into electronic markets and spurring growth of dark pools and high-frequency trading.

2006

The X factor.
Instinet launches Chi-X Europe to little fanfare; within five years, it becomes the largest pan-European trading platform.

2007

Good timing.
As the original Markets in Financial Instruments Directive is being rolled out (just prior to the Financial Crisis) Instinet joins the Nomura family, retaining separate, agency model execution brokerages focused on execution quality.

The Two Thousand Tens

2012

Well executed.
As global regulations impacting liquidity, risk provision, and order management increase market complexity, Instinet becomes the global execution arm of Nomura.

2014

Turn up the volume.
More than 75 percent of the stocks traded on US exchanges originate from automated systems.

2015

Keep calm and carry on.
In anticipation of MiFID II unbundling rules, Instinet Europe Limited becomes the first broker to register with the FCA as a payment institution.

2016

Independent thinkers.
Instinet brings the US Equity Research team over from Nomura, establishing a new MiFID II-ready research boutique offering.

2017

Block party.
Instinet acquires BlockCross ATS, adding another unique block trading capability to its platform.

2018

The new normal?
MiFID II arrives, reshaping the European financial regulatory landscape and fundamentally changing the flow of liquidity and relationships between market participants.

2019

Fintech@Fifty
Insinet celebrates its role as the original “fintech” and 50 years as a transformative force in the markets.
