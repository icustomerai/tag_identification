OUR STORY

Sigma is a global company born in Mexico, our purpose is to bring communities everywhere favorite foods to love. With presence in 18 countries and more than 50 brands, we provide quality products in perishable categories, such as packaged deli meats and cheeses.

Mexican Cheese Producers Inc was established in 1994, the original founder from Guanajuato, Mexico, began producing 60lb wheels of authentic Cotija to be sold in the Chicago market, a strong demand for his cheese encouraged diversification into multiple Mexican and Caribbean cheeses, and eventually led to the construction of a brand new production plant in Darlington, Wisconsin. A prime location with access to the finest quality milk in the country.

Our flagship Cotija cheese recently won the 1st Place best of class at the 2020 World Championship Cheese contest, joining our Panela Cheese which won 1st place in the 2019 US Championship, other cheeses we carry are Fresco, Quesadilla, Blanco, Chihuahua and a wide variety of creams.

In Mexico, Sigma has the #1 Market Share position, not only we are experts in Mexican Cheeses, but we are also able to produce and import directly from our Jalisco, Mexico plant. We follow artisanal cheesemaking techniques to hand braid our Oaxaca Cheese, the finest available in the US. Besides producing the highest quality, authentic cheeses and creams, Sigma is constantly developing cheese innovations and exploring new packaging technologies. We are committed to offering only the finest quality products on the market.

FRESCO

Fresco is the most popular cheese used in Mexican cooking. It is a high moisture cheese with a crumbly texture and milky flavor. It does not melt when heated. You will find a wide variety of Frescos ranging from higher moisture to lower moisture and mild to strong flavors. It is made from Pasteurized Cow’s milk.

Attributes and Advantages:

• Meaning ‘fresh cheese’ in Spanish, our milky white, subtly salty crumbling queso fresco provides the perfect balance for Mexican recipes.

• For our Fresco cheeses, we have different options ranging in moisture and cream, allowing us to offer a targeted solution to different applications or regional preferences.

• Our authentic Mexican-style crumbling cheese enhances everything from salads and tamales to tacos and enchiladas.

• It can also be referred as Queso de Rancho, Ranchero, Sierra and Canasto.

• You can also substitute Queso Fresco for any recipes calling for goat cheese, feta, or ricotta.

• Easily crumble by putting a chunk of the cheese in a sealed bag and massage the bag until it is crumbly. Another way is to gently break off pieces of the cheese with a fork or with your fingers.

COTIJA

Cotija is a cow’s milk hard cheese that is dry and crumbly with a resemblance to feta cheese. It’s a salty cheese with a distinctive strong flavor.

Attributes and Advantages:

• Our Signature Cheese, Cotija, has won 7 awards over the years. Including Best of Class at the World Championship Cheese Contest. It is crafted in 60lb wheels and Conveniently grated and packed ready to use, it will save you time in the kitchen.

• Some Cotija cheeses have an extremely strong aroma, our Cotija is balanced to perfection.

• Cotija cheese originated in the Mexican state of Michoacán in the town of Cotija. It is still being produced some 400 years after it was first introduced.

• The grated version of cotija cheese resembles Parmesan cheese and can be used in the same way: sprinkle some on top of pasta, in tacos, or in soups. Extremely popular with “Street corn”.

OAXACA

Oaxaca is a pasta-filata cheese that is a staple in Mexican cooking and similar to string cheese or mozzarella. An authentic Oaxaca is a string cheese that is braided into a ball. It has a mild flavor and is great for melting.

Attributes and Advantages:

• Our Oaxaca is imported from Mexico, we hand braid this cheese in our plant in Lagos de Moreno, Jalisco. It is the finest Oaxaca available in the USA.

• Oaxaca cheese is named for Oaxaca, a state in Southern Mexico. It is also known as Quesillo.

• Mellow and buttery, great for quesadillas, or use it with chorizo for queso fundido or for stuffing chiles rellenos.

PANELA

Panela is another popular fresh cheese with a mild flavor. It is white in color similar to Fresco; however, the texture is not crumbly and it’s less salty. Great for cooking, as its mellow flavor soaks up flavors of other ingredients. Extremely popular in Mexico as a table cheese, and a top choice as ingredient for authentic Mexican cuisine.

Attributes and Advantages:

• Our Award-Winning Panela formula comes directly from the source, we invited one of our Cheesemakers from Mexico to move to Wisconsin to make this fabulous cheese, using the finest milk in the country.

• Queso Panela originated in central Mexico. It is commonly referred to as Queso Canasta, which literally translates to “basket cheese,” because it is molded in baskets.

• Though Panela is not a melting cheese, it is quite versatile. It can be used as table cheese or in fresh applications like salads, it can be pan fried or grilled, or baked and used as ingredient.

• It is a great substitute for any Queso Blanco application.

MELTING CHEESES

These are specially used for melting, such as Chihuahua cheese and Quesadilla cheese

Chihuahua:

• Named after the Mennonite farmers from Chihuahua, Mexico, this cheese is slightly yellow, that has a sharper but mild, buttery taste.

• Its flavors intensify with age and become tangier and sharper over time.

• Similar in taste and texture to Monterey Jack, allowing for easy substitutions in your favorite cheesy dishes.

• The firm, yet supple texture creates a soft, melting cheese that’s perfect for meals such as queso fundido, chiles rellenos, tamales, lasagna, casseroles and more.

Quesadilla:

• Quesadilla Cheese is not really a Mexican cheese, you won’t find it in Mexico. It was born in the USA with the popularity of Quesadillas, though often, Oaxaca or Chihuahua cheese is used as the basis for quesadillas.

• Quesadillas originated in the southern and central regions of Mexico. Authentic corn quesadillas are cooked on a flat griddle called a comal and are stuffed with cheese and perhaps meat or vegetables. In the Northern parts of Mexico Flour tortillas are more popular.

• Ideal to use in any dishes calling for melted cheese. Besides quesadillas, use it as the base for Mexican pizza, a grilled cheese sandwich, or make a Mexican cheese dip to serve at parties.

MEXICAN CREAMS

Acidified Mexican style sour cream is closer in texture and consistency to crème fraîche (a heavy cream regularly used in French cuisine) than it is to conventional Cultured Sour Cream.

Attributes and Advantages:

• Our recipe has an ideal blend of tangy and sour with a hint of salt, can be used to balance some of the spicier flavors in traditional Mexican cuisine.

• Silky and velvety, its texture is uncommonly smooth and rich tasting. It can be dipped with Tortilla Chips without breaking.

• It’s a delicious substitute for sour cream and can work as a garnish or condiment. It can also be featured in sweet applications such as a favorite Mexican dessert: Strawberries and Cream.

CHORIZOS

Chorizo refers to spicy sausage and is a staple in Mexican, Spanish, and Latin American food.

Attributes and Advantages:

• Toluca, not far from Mexico City, is not only the capital of the State of Mexico, but for centuries has been known as the Chorizo Capital of Mexico.

• What distinguishes Mexican Chorizo from other Latin American or Spanish Chorizo is the type of added seasoning; Mexican Chorizo is generally the spiciest, usually containing a combination of ground chile peppers such as ancho or chipotle, along with other seasonings.

• Another way to tell Mexican Chorizo from its counterparts is its trademark reddish tinge.

• Chorizo is versatile and can be blended into many dishes, such as in tomato sauce, taco meat, on pizza, as a stuffing for pork roast, or, as a kickstart to your morning when added to breakfast burritos.
