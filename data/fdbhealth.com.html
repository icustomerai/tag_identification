ABOUT US

We Turn Drug Data into Drug Knowledge that Millions Rely On

Where does the data come from and what do we do with it?

FDB’s drug databases are compiled by highly trained and experienced clinical staff from the most relevant sources of drug product information, such as the FDA and trusted medical and pharmacy journals. Our team of pharmacists critically review, synthesize and apply their own expertise to the drug data, as guided by editorial policy.

What do software developers do with FDB's drug databases?

Information system developers and self-programmers – from those building electronic medical records and pharmacy dispensing systems, to patient portals, mobile apps, telemedicine and hundreds of other decision support systems – program to our drug databases and build software that supports medication decision making by their users.

Who relies on FDB’s drug databases and how do they use it?

Millions of people – including physicians, nurses, pharmacists, claims adjudicators, and patients – rely on our drug databases, as distributed through our trusted partners, to help them choose, dose, prescribe, and use medications properly. Precise medication information is critical to help ensure the best possible health outcomes.
