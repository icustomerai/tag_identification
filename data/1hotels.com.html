Sanctuary Stories

1 Hotel Brooklyn Bridge

Take a seat that tells a story. Much of the furniture in our sanctuary is constructed with reclaimed wood from Brooklyn’s old Domino Sugar Factory. 

Learn More
Sanctuary Stories

1 Hotel Central Park

36,000 ivy plants blanket our urban sanctuary, each working to produce oxygen, absorb greenhouse gasses, reduce surface temperatures in the summer, and insulate in the winter. 

Learn More
Sanctuary Stories

1 Hotel Hanalei Bay

Throughout our island oasis, you'll find 144.6 tons of salvaged materials turned into furniture and sustainable art pieces that pay homage to their surroundings.

Learn More
Sanctuary Stories

1 Hotel Mayfair

Connect with the past while enjoying the present as our dry stone walls in the reception and signature bar are formed from reclaimed stone walls in Yorkshire, brought down from the north of England and built by hand using traditional techniques. 

Sanctuary Stories

1 Hotel Nashville

You’ll find over 56,000 individual plants in our ivy façade, capturing CO2, filtering airborne particulates, absorbing heat from sidewalks, and generating fresh oxygen.

Learn More
Sanctuary Stories

1 Hotel San Francisco

Our lobby flooring is composed of 7,000 square feet of reclaimed barn wood, with our elevator landings reclaimed from the old Bay Bridge. This design detail allowed us to divert landfill waste, cut carbon emissions, and prevent the harvesting of virgin wood. 

Learn More

1 Hotel & Homes South Beach

Throughout our sanctuary, we’ve rescued and repurposed 7,000 square feet of hardwood from boardwalks and pool decks to offset the purchasing and shipping footprints of new materials. 

Learn More

1 Hotel Toronto

Local woodworking studio Just Be Woodsy transformed fallen Toronto trees into over 1,000 pieces of hand-crafted, one-of-a-kind furnishings throughout our urban sanctuary. 

Learn More

1 Hotel West Hollywood

The carpets throughout our Southern California sanctuary are made of recycled materials including ocean plastics, helping to divert unnecessary waste from oceans and landfills.
